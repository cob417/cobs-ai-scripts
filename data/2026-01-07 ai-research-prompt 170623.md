SECTION 1 — Emerging Themes (3–5)

1) AI moves from “capability race” to ROI and consolidation  
The narrative is shifting from bigger/better models to measurable business outcomes, with boards demanding proof that AI spend converts into productivity or revenue. This is also driving consolidation: enterprises and investors expect fewer vendors to win bigger contracts as pilots turn into standardized stacks.  
Who’s saying it: business outlets; venture investors; enterprise executives

2) Agentic AI hits the “production reality” wall (infra, orchestration, reliability)  
Enterprises are discovering that agents fail less because of model IQ and more because of missing foundations: identity, permissions, knowledge access, observability, and safe tool execution. Practitioner discussions are increasingly about architectures (e.g., mesh vs orchestration) and operational discipline, not demos.  
Who’s saying it: Fortune 500 engineering teams; technical reporters; platform builders

3) Interoperability becomes a first-order agent platform requirement  
As organizations deploy multiple agents across apps and clouds, standardized agent-to-agent communication is emerging as a prerequisite to scale beyond isolated pilots. Protocol thinking (and alignment with tool-connection standards like MCP) is becoming a core “plumbing” topic.  
Who’s saying it: developer advocates; enterprise platform teams; cloud ecosystem partners

4) AI security pivots to agent governance (permissions, identity, and autonomous action controls)  
Security leaders are expanding AI usage in cyber ops while simultaneously treating agents as a new attack surface—because agents can take actions, touch systems, and move laterally if mis-scoped. The emphasis is on guardrails, monitoring, and “human-in-the-loop” boundaries for high-risk operations.  
Who’s saying it: security leaders; business reporters; enterprise risk teams
